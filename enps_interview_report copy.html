At the start of 2026, we analyzed the results of our eNPS survey, a regular check-in about how we're doing at the company and what we'd like to improve. Nastya, our HR lead, sat down with @nata and answered 10 questions — it turned out to be something like a mini-investigation.

You prepared the eNPS analytics with Antigravity. How long did it take you to write the initial prompt?

— I'm a lazy person, so in this case I opened ChatGPT and simply voiced what data I had and what result I wanted to get. For me, this is easier than writing everything by hand.

Then I checked that ChatGPT understood me correctly and didn't add any nonsense, made some edits—and in 10-15 minutes, I had a structured prompt.

The eNPS score dropped by almost 19% over the year. What's the reason?

The eNPS score—our main metric—is derived from answers to one question: 'How likely are you to recommend Alconost as a place of work for your friends?' Our result for 2025 is 63%.

For the IT niche, a good indicator is 50%; our 63% is pretty solid. But I know that Alconost can do even better.

The decline is connected to the growth of those who rated their willingness to recommend at 1-3 points. In previous years it was less than 1%, but in 2025 it was already 7–8%.

I understand the reasons: frozen benefits and salary increases, unstable situation. The drop tells me that people answered honestly: eNPS reflected the real situation, not a rosy picture.

3
Only 5% anonymous responses—is that a lot or a little?

— Generally, in the IT industry, surveys like this are anonymous by default. We've always conducted them non-anonymously so that we could work constructively with the feedback: come to a specific person, discuss concerns, and improve the situation.

But in 2025, against the backdrop of frozen salary increases and benefits, it seemed important to give people the option to speak up anonymously.

In reality, only 6 out of 115 answers were anonymous. I think that's not much.

This experience confirmed that we have a generally healthy culture, because even in difficult times, the absolute majority feels comfortable sharing their opinions openly.

5
But at the same time, the percentage of satisfaction with tasks actually increased. Why?

I connect this to a broader context. The world and the market as a whole have changed; not just our company and not just the localization industry got "shaken up." Perhaps, observing how things are going for others, we simply started to cherish more what we have.

We managed to build a pretty healthy system: negativity doesn't accumulate, problems get discussed, solutions are found.

To the question "Do you feel that your eNPS feedback has ever helped improve your work experience or any processes at Alconost?" three-quarters of people—77%—noted a positive effect.

And in the end, despite external factors, we remain healthy and motivated inside.

The era when IT companies competed with offices, cookies, and latte syrups is gone. Much more important are a healthy atmosphere, team, and culture. We've always stood out in this way.

You can attract people to a company with flexible schedules and remote work, but what makes people want to stay in a company—that's precisely team and culture.

98 responses mentioned team and culture, and only 30 mentioned remote & flexibility. Culture is hard to formalize, but it's deeply embedded.

Your video was recorded on January 4th at 10:56 PM. Why are you working on holidays at night?

I'm a pretty creative person, and that part of me lives on its own schedule—I don't know how to tame my own inspiration. If it came on January 4th at 10 PM—well, who am I to argue with it?

Sun Jan 4  22:56

When something lights me up, I follow that fire. That's not burnout — it's fuel.

Following the fire and burning out at work are different, completely different things.

At the same time, my work-life balance doesn't suffer. I clearly understand what I can do, when, and how much. And if I feel tired from creative evenings like these, I'll not keep doing the same. But this is personally how it works for me—after all, everyone has their own biorhythms and their own schedule of inspiration surges.

10 / 10
10
Your overall impression of eNPS-2025? Main goals for HR based on the results?

The eNPS results overall match reality. Even considering that not everyone fills out the survey and we now send out the questionnaire a bit less frequently—I see that our compass is working.

The most unexpected thing for me personally was the result of the experiment with the anonymous option. On one hand, I didn't get the effect I expected. On the other hand—if the anonymous option isn't in demand, then we really don't need it that much after all.

I'm all for sincere and constructive feedback not only in eNPS, but in daily work too.

A healthy culture isn't about staying silent or having discussions in the hallways, but about open dialogue and the ability to speak up with the desire to make things better. This is about our collective growth as a team and as a company.
